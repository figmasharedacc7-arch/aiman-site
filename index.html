Chapter One

The Long Game.

I started in promotional marketing in 1985. Then I built the next venture, and the one after that, and the one after that. Construction. Advertising. Engineering. AI. Growth advisory. Seven companies, four decades, one operating philosophy that has not changed: build the rails before you run the train.

The work that lasts is rarely the work that trends. It is the work you keep refining when no one is watching, the relationships you keep tending when there is nothing on the table, the standards you hold when convenience would be cheaper.

Compounding is the only edge that does not get arbitraged away. Show up, do the work, repeat for forty years.

I write about it now because the lessons matter more than the wins. The wins were idiosyncratic. The lessons travel.

Aiman Vancouver, BC

The Operating System

Four frameworks I keep coming back to.

A working blueprint. Refined over forty years of building, breaking, and rebuilding.

01

Dragon Leadership

Composure under pressure. Bias for the long arc. Authority that does not need volume. The best operators I know move slowly, then all at once.

02

Strategy as Outcome

The deck is not the work. The plan is not the work. Outcomes are the work. Every meeting, every quarter, every initiative gets graded on the same rubric: did the needle move?

03

Decision Infrastructure

In a noisy world, clarity is leverage. Build the rails that make every choice cheaper, faster, and harder to regret. Then let the system carry the load.

04

GTM Architecture

Pipelines, plays, and revenue paths designed end to end, then run like a factory. Growth is engineered, not summoned. Predictability beats heroics every quarter.

Career Arc

Forty years, in milestones.

A condensed timeline. The companies, the bets, the lessons that shaped the next decade.

1985

Founded Aiman El-Ramly Promotional Group

Started in promotional marketing in Vancouver. The first venture. The first proof that building beats waiting.

1990s

Scaled into creative and brand work

Founded AE Creative. Worked across hospitality, retail, and civic clients. Learned the difference between making things look good and making things actually work.

2000s

Crossed into construction and engineering

Operating partner on infrastructure projects. Hard hats and balance sheets. The decade that shaped the operating mindset that still governs everything.

2015

Founded ZECO Engineering

Industrial engineering for the next generation of infrastructure. The marriage of construction discipline and modern engineering.

2018

FourGO and the travel platform years

Built and ran a travel discovery platform for the Pacific Northwest. The lesson: distribution is everything, and channels compound when you stop chasing tactics.

2022

Founded OmniGTM.ai and Nexsun.ai

Two AI ventures. One for go-to-market intelligence. One for marketing automation. The frameworks of forty years, encoded into software.

2025

Real Success Machine and the mentorship cohort

The platform for the philosophy. Events, content, mentorship for founders past product-market fit. The work I should have been doing for the last decade.

Reading List

What I point people toward.

A short list of books I keep buying for founders. Not the trendy ones. The ones that actually held up.

  • The Effective ExecutivePeter Drucker
    The original operator's handbook. Read it once a year for the rest of your career.
  • Good to GreatJim Collins
    Less famous than its title would suggest. The chapter on the flywheel alone earns the book.
  • The Hard Thing About Hard ThingsBen Horowitz
    When the deck does not match the reality, this is the book.
  • High Output ManagementAndrew Grove
    The clearest writing on operating cadence ever written. Still unmatched.
  • Letters from a StoicSeneca
    Two thousand years old and still the best writing on composure I have ever read.
